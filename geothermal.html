Geothermal Energy

Cost

Geothermal heating and cooling systems work by circulating water underground for your house to use to control the temperature. The heating system works by pumping water or another fluid through pipes underground where it absorbs heat and is then circulated through the house to heat it. The fluid drops in temperature while heating your house and is then circulated back into the ground to be heated again. The cooling system works the same way except the water is heated in your house then cooled underground. This system takes advantage of the ground’s natural tendency to change temperature more slowly than the air.

Information

To install a Geothermal heating and cooling system, you first need to drill wells into the ground which may cost between $10,000 and $30,000 depending on soil type (rocky or hard soil will cost more because of the difficulty drilling into them). You can then expect to pay between $10,000 and $20,000 to install the system.

Tax rebate information

Through the inflation reduction act, you can receive 30% tax credit by purchasing a system that has at least 3 kWh of battery storage capacity.

Clean/green energy practices

By investing in a geothermal heating and cooling system for your home or business, you decrease the amount of natural gas consumed, thereby reducing the carbon and other emissions released into the atmosphere every year.
